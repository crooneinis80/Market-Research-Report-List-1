Ion exchange resins utilize charge-based interactions for effective separation of ions, while hydrophobic resins function by repelling water, targeting non-polar compounds in biochemical contexts. Hydroxyapatite resins mimic biological mineral structures, facilitating the selective binding of proteins and biomolecules. Together, these mixed-mode resins enhance versatility and efficiency in chromatography, catering to diverse industries such as pharmaceuticals and biotechnology.</p></p>
